Background

In addition to software development and arts education and management, I have extensive experience as a playwright, in North Carolina and nationally. I earned my MFA in Dramatic Writing from NYU in 2004, where I also won the Harry Kondoleon Award in playwriting, along with the Kennedy Center's 2002 Jean Kennedy Smith Award for The Nature of the Nautilus.

Works

From 2004 until 2012, I served as Resident Playwright for Burning Coal Theatre Company, a small professional theater in Raleigh, NC. Notable works for Burning Coal include 1960, written to celebrate the anniversary of the desegregation of Raleigh schools, Jude the Obscure, a two-part adaptation of Thomas Hardy's masterpiece, and The Greeks, an adaptaion of Sophocles' Theban Plays. Other major works include Native for EBZB Productions and And There Was War in Heaven, selected as a Finalist in the 2017 O'Neil National Playwright's Conference.

Awards

For this body of work, I was named the Piedmont Laureate in Playwriting and Screenwriting in 2012 by the Arts Councils of central North Carolina. In addition, for my work on the Our Histories series, dramatizing the history of Raleigh, I was included in Legendary Locals of Raleigh (Arcadia Publishing, 2013).
